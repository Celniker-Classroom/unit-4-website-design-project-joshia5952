Iowa is home to one of the world’s largest frying pans, measuring roughly 14 feet. Located in Brandon, this frying pan will have you in awe over its frying capabilities. With this much fryerpower, you can fry corn to your heart’s content. The creativity of the Iowan people is unmatched, and this landmark is proof of that. The 1500 pound frying pan was built in 2004 to promote Brandon’s annual “Cowboy Breakfast” fundraiser. Since then, the frying pan has become the pride and joy of all 300 people living in Brandon, and showcases the grit and determination of all Iowans. Also, you may be wondering, why is it only Iowa's largest frying pan, and not the world's? Who else would be stupid (determined) enough to build a frying pan in contention for the world's largest? Well, the humility of the Iowan people knows no bounds, and while building the frying pan there was a mismeasurement (deliberate decision) that made the frying pan 3 inches smaller than the world's largest at the time, in order to showcase the modesty of the Iowan people.
